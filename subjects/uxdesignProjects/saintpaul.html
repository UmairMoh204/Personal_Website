Organization

The City of Saint Paul is a city that radiates with rich history, tradition, diversity, and economic vitality. The city has a strong commitment to preserving and enhancing the urban villages of its residents with the likes of the thriving Downtown area and its revitalized riverfront.

The City of Saint Paul plays an immense role as a commercial hub with many Fortune 500 companies such as 3M, Target, EcoLab and many more with numerous small businesses playing an immense role in the city's economy.

The City of Saint Paul is not only the seat of government for Minnesota but also a cultural heart and a city that many of its residents call home. The City of Saint Paul offers numerous opportunities for its residents such as Public Safety, Community Development, Educational Support, and much more.

Analyzing & Testing Landscape

Upon my arrival as the UX Research Intern for the Human Resources team within the City of Saint Paul, I was met with previous research regarding the updates for the new Human Resources page. The team had completed and organized the user reviews of the existing Human Resource pages as well as numerous rounds of Usability tests on the existing Human Resources pages.

One serious problem that the team was facing was creating a strategy for organizing a significant number of subpages into neatly compacted and very efficient structure to create an improved user experience. I was given the responsibility of creating an efficient navigation for the Human Resources section of the City of Saint Paul. After discussions with the team, we decided that I would conduct a Card Sorting Activity and create an Information Architecture and Site Map from the research gathered from the activity.

Open Card Sorting Activity

Before we began our Card Sorting Activity, we began first analyzing what kind of Card Sorting Activity we would conduct. We had to make a decision between Open Card Sorting Activity and Closed Card Sorting Activity in order to observe the user's mental map of an recognizable user interface.

In order to meet the needs of the team and observe user groups we decided to implement a Open Card Sorting Activity in order to best evaluate the participants groupings and observe their psychological process of grouping the cards and giving participants the freedom to explore and expand on their process of grouping the cards. This gives us a stronger insight on the participants process in order to develop an Information Architecture and Site Map that best reflects the participants cognitive model of an ideal Human Resource section of the City of Saint Paul page.

The Open Card Sorting Activity was conducted on 15 participants with majority of which live within Saint Paul. This pool of applicants allowed us to gain insight on the Human Resources pages for our targeted audience allowing us to take direct insight of individuals who lived within the city of Saint Paul.

Challenges

  • Organizing a large number of subpages into an efficient navigation structure that would serve diverse user groups
  • Balancing the needs of different stakeholders while maintaining a user-centered approach to information architecture
  • Ensuring the new navigation structure would be intuitive for both internal employees and external users accessing HR resources

Next Steps

  • Conduct follow-up usability testing on the implemented navigation structure to validate the card sorting results
  • Expand the research to include other city departments to create a consistent navigation pattern across the entire website
  • Develop a comprehensive content strategy to ensure all HR information is properly categorized and easily discoverable
